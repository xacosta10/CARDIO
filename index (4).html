The Information Cycle

When you find a source, it helps to understand where it falls in the information cycle. A social media post is very different from a peer-reviewed journal article. Different assignments call for different source types, so knowing how information develops over time will help you make better choices.

The Information Cycle: how information develops over time, from social media posts to scholarly research.

The Peer Review Cycle

You will encounter the term peer-reviewed throughout this tutorial. Peer-reviewed sources are evaluated by other experts before being published and are among the most credible types of sources for academic research. The diagram below shows how that process works.

The peer review process: from research to publication.

What is CARDIO?

Not all sources are equally trustworthy. The CARDIO method is a tool to help you evaluate whether a source is appropriate and credible to use in your work.

Each letter stands for a criterion to evaluate when looking at a source. In this tutorial, you will learn what each letter means, see real examples, and answer a question before moving on.

The practice source is a blog article titled The Effects of Caffeine Consumption Among College Students.

The CARDIO evaluation framework.

Lateral Reading

One of the most important skills in evaluating sources is lateral reading. Instead of reading deeply into a source, you open new tabs and search for what others say about the author or organization. Watch the video below to see it in action.

Bias Exists Everywhere: Even reputable sources have perspectives. Recognize bias rather than pretend it does not exist. Using a variety of sources with different viewpoints strengthens your research.
Types of media bias to be aware of when evaluating sources for objectivity.

Media bias appears in many forms. Understanding the difference between reader bias, like confirmation bias, and source bias, like corporate or political bias, helps you evaluate whether a source is presenting information fairly or pushing an agenda.
